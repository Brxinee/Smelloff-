What makes a fabric spray actually work

Three things. If any one is missing, the product won't hold up:

  1. A real odor-neutralising active. Zinc PCA, cyclodextrin, or chlorine dioxide. These chemically bind to odor molecules. Without one of these, a "fabric spray" is just perfume in water — it masks for 90 minutes and the odor comes back.
  2. A clean, fabric-safe carrier. Water base with a small amount of IPA or ethanol for fast drying. No heavy oils (they stain), no strong dyes, pH 5.5–6.5.
  3. A format that matches the use case. Home use = 250ml+. Pocket/gym/office use = 50ml. If the bottle doesn't fit where you need it, you won't use it. Most Indian buyers need pocket format because the odor problem happens on the go.

The scam to watch for: "Fabric fresheners" and "clothing perfumes" sold for ₹150–500 on quick-commerce apps rarely list an odor-neutralising active. They're fragrance + alcohol + water. They smell nice for an hour. They don't remove odor.

Room fresheners and air sprays are not fabric sprays. Products like Lia, Odonil, and Air Wick are designed for airborne dispersal in rooms.
